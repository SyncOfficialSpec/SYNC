SYNC

Devlog 4 • July 22, 2026

Three big additions this time: a real sign in screen, a Joiner app for hopping into servers, and an experimental Desktop mode that turns the whole screen into a Mac desktop. Plus a fix that finally turned shadows on everywhere.

New Elements:
Sign In Screen
SYNC now opens with a proper sign in, right after the boot splash. You type a username and password (the password shows as dots), hit LOG IN, and the button smoothly turns from purple to green and shows a little loading spinner. Then a security panel fades in on the right and runs through a few checks one by one, Windows or macOS, Anti Virus, Driver Blacklist and Driver Check, each spinning and then ticking off. Once it clears, a verify screen slides in with your avatar, your name and UID, and a set of animated bars while it finishes. A wrong login turns the button red and shakes it. The whole thing runs over a blurred, dimmed backdrop.

Joiner
A new dock app with two tools. The top half copies an invite link to the exact server you are in right now, so a friend can drop straight into your game. The bottom half is the reverse: paste anyone's game or server link, hit Join, and it teleports you into their server. Free users generate a SYNC code that only the SYNC Joiner can read, while a premium unlock gives a normal link that works anywhere. It shows the game name and player count, and the whole thing has proper icons and gradient buttons.

Desktop mode
An experimental toggle in Settings that turns the entire screen into a macOS style desktop. It uses your real wallpaper, adds a menu bar with a live clock across the top, and frames the running game inside a Mac window in the middle. Flip it on to feel like the game is just another app on your desktop, and off to go back to normal.

Improvements:
The sign in detects your real device and names it in the first security check instead of guessing.
Loading is slower and smoother now, with softer easing so nothing snaps.
When the security panel comes in, the background gently dims behind it so the cards stand out.
Real drop shadows now show up across the whole app, on windows and cards, giving everything more depth.
The Joiner invite link points at the exact server instance, not just the game, so friends land next to you.
Window corners stay nicely rounded even while panels slide in over them.
Fixed the login cards showing blank, the corners going sharp mid animation, and the button turning red on a correct login.
